It has already been 1.5 years into graduate school life,
which means 2 years remain until graduation.
2 years left to chase my HCI research dream and go on adventures.

I cannot foresee who I will become in the future,
but I know — I simply love living in the present moment.

Practicing small talk with a teacher from the Philippines — a skill he spent 10 years mastering.
Learning Taiwanese and Thai alongside my Thai friends.
Dreaming with my Chinese friends of one day creating something new together.
Exploring design iteration with the professors and students at SoftLab.
Staying up late with senior lab mates, writing papers — savoring what it means to be the junior.
Embracing the ocean, exploring sea slugs all by myself.
Making peace with my past self, and embracing the unknown.
Accepting every change that arrives in my life.

One day, when I look back on all of this,
I will miss these beautiful moments.
Perhaps just a simple, ordinary instant.
Perhaps just another everyday afternoon.
Maybe when I recall the professor's daily nagging and watchful eye, I'll crack a smile.
But by that day, none of it will belong to the same person you were back then —
it will belong to a more complete version of you.
