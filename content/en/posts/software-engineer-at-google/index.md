## Knowledge Sharing

* Emphasizes knowledge sharing to avoid information silos. Every isolated silo gives rise to a problem, so building a knowledge base within the team is an important first step.
* In team learning, building a psychologically safe environment for less experienced team members is important.


### Team Interaction Patterns

  | Recommended Patterns | Anti-Patterns |
| -------- | -------- |
| Basic questions or mistakes are guided in the right direction | Basic questions are called out, and the person asking is punished |
| Explanations are given to help the questioner learn | Explanations are given to show off |
 | Responses are friendly, patient, and helpful | Responses are arrogant, sarcastic, and unconstructive |
 | Interactions are collaborative discussions aimed at finding solutions | Interactions are arguments designed to produce "winners" and "losers" |

### Closing Thoughts
* Start small: ask questions and write things down.
* At the system level, encourage and reward those who take time to teach and expand their expertise for the benefit of others, their team, and the organization - not just for themselves.


## How to Lead a Team?

> Traditional managers worry about how to complete tasks, while great managers worry about what tasks will be completed (and trust their team to figure out how to solve them).

### Failure Is an Option

Failure is acceptable; failure is a fast way to learn (provided you don't fail at the same thing repeatedly). View failure as a learning opportunity rather than something to blame.
* Failing fast is good because it doesn't carry excessive risk.
* In the event of failure, seeking individual accountability is a good way to divide the team and discourage taking risks overall.
* Individual success => public recognition; failure => constructive feedback in private.
* Whatever happens, seize the opportunity and use humility, respect, and trust to help your team learn from failure.

### Anti-Patterns

#### Hiring Weaklings
* Don't see strong people as a threat; instead, surround yourself with people who are smarter than you.
* This way, expanding your own expertise becomes much easier.

#### Ignoring Low Performers

Ignoring low performers:
* Prevents new high performers from joining your team and encourages existing high performers to leave.
* Ultimately the team becomes filled entirely with low performers.

The benefit of getting to know low performers quickly is:
* You may discover they just need a little encouragement to do very well.

How to coach low performers?

* Respect - a great deal of respect, humility, and trust.
* Set a specific timeframe with very specific goals, and expect them to achieve these within the timeframe.
* Set goals that are measurable, small in scale, and incremental.

Meet weekly to check progress:
* Ensure clear expectations are set for each upcoming milestone.


#### Becoming Everyone's Friend

Because leaders don't want to lose the team friendships they've worked hard to build, they work to preserve them => leading to disaster.
* You can lead a team and build consensus without needing to be friends.
* Leadership does not equal gentleness and friendship.
