# Regressing to a Primitive State

While doing my literature review,
I realized my writing and argumentation had degraded to the point where
I needed to type out fragmented notes
and ask AI to assemble them into sentences for me.
I would read them, understand them, then go back and revise.

I started wondering — the day I cancel my AI subscription,
what will be left that is truly my own voice?
My advisor reminded me too: my own voice matters.

So what should I do?
I recalled a book a senior lab mate once mentioned to me.
## "712 Things to Write About"
I rushed to the library like a madman
and finally found it on the sixth floor.

On the walk home,
I opened a conversation with my own voice.
If I deleted AI, would my voice still be audible?
Obviously, these are two questions that cannot be placed side by side.

Back to the literature review —
I knew what I needed to do.
Link my ideas in Obsidian,
just like *Linkography: Unfolding the Design Process* —
connect the dots and that's it.

## The Professor's Advice — Reclaiming My Own Voice

In repeated iteration,
a system is not born from completion,
but from revision.

The first time is just a simulation,
like tracing the outline of emotions amid the chaos;
few samples,
yet enough to prove
it was once seen.

The second time,
the formula begins to flow.
The flow of life is written into computation,
OpenCV begins real-time recognition,
AI no longer waits for humans to click on emotions —
it tries to understand on its own.

The third time,
the goal is not perfection
but stability.
Every delay, misclassification, and dropout
becomes truly important evidence
inside the thesis.

And so you understand:
research is not about proving you were never wrong,
but about leaving behind
the reason for every correction.

After four rounds of testing,
the system took shape;
a conversation between two people
made the questions run even deeper.

Between designer and engineer,
it's not just a difference in language —
it's a difference in how they understand the world.

And emotion
has never been a single signal.
It hides in the voice,
rests in the expression,
and pulses between heartbeats.

Multimodality is not the subject being validated —
it is simply the way AI learns to understand people;
like a ruler,
letting machines take over the tedious calculations from humans,
leaving more space
for genuine thinking and communication.

What you built is not a finished product.
What you built is an MVP
that is still breathing.

It may not yet be vast,
the smartwatch integration may not be complete,
but it is already enough to prove
that a cross-domain emotion computing system
can be built, revised, and continued.

And what the thesis leaves behind in the end
is not only architecture and data.

It is also every decision you made
through countless failures.

# How to Start a Literature Review?
Here are the tools I found:

### Define the focus of your topic — filter your literature by first defining the scope of your thesis, then use AI tools to select relevant papers and avoid searching blindly
- Scispace: Quickly grasp the key points of each paper, compile a literature review table, and interact with papers via AI
https://scispace.com/?via=study
- Consensus: Filter literature by research type and organize key points
https://consensus.app/
- Connected Papers: Visualize the relationship graph between papers and discover other important references
https://www.connectedpapers.com/

### Quickly grasp English literature
Translate target papers, skim for key points, and decide whether they can be used in your writing
https://immersivetranslate.com/en/?via=study

### Import filtered literature, provide your thesis topic, and generate a thesis outline
- Google Notebook LM
Can be used for brainstorming actual writing points; content is grounded in the imported literature and does not "hallucinate" nonexistent information.
It can also generate a two-person dialogue podcast for audio-based learning, though currently only available in English.
https://notebooklm.google.com/

### Centralize literature management and write the first draft
Manage first-draft notes in an organized manner — jot down the main ideas and cited references first
https://obsidian.md/
Common plugin: Obsidian PDF++
Based on the AI-generated outline and key points, copy relevant passages from different papers into a blank document, add your own perspective and annotations, and make sure the content meets requirements. The PDF++ plugin is recommended — it lets you quickly jump back to the original location in the source text.

### Refine the draft: ChatGPT o1
Don't obsess over grammar and phrasing in the first draft — letting AI clean up the English later will save a huge amount of time!
It makes the expression throughout the entire paper much smoother.

### Organize citations
Common tool: Zotero
Most people are familiar with this one — the Google browser extension is extremely convenient. You can add papers to your database as you search, automatically generate a reference list, and then proofread it yourself.
https://www.zotero.org/

I also personally prefer writing papers in Overleaf using LaTeX.
