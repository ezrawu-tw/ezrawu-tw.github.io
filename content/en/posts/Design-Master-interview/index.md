# NYUST Interview Reflection

Three familiar professors greeted me: TWC, CMW, and LSL.

**TWC:** *“Keep it concise. I have a lot of questions for you.” 😈*  
**CMW:** *“Besides my class and TWC’s, which course impressed you the most?”*  
**EZRA:** OMO Marketing — its service design process aligns with my project cycles.

**TWC:** *“After entering the master’s program, what kind of person do you expect to become?”*  
**EZRA:**  
> An entrepreneur with strong communication skills.  
> In the past, my sharpness sometimes pushed others away.  
> The teams I led consisted of elites — IIT, University of Adelaide, University of South Africa.  
> They were self-driven; what they needed was direction and efficiency.

**LSL:** *“Why transition from Information Management to Design? Are you graduating this year?”*  
**EZRA:**  
> In the era of AI, the world doesn’t lack engineers — it lacks architects:  
> people who can orchestrate systems and make technology truly usable.  
> Overspecialization is becoming a liability; it makes us replaceable.  
> I want to shift from result-oriented thinking to process-oriented design thinking.  
> And yes, I’m in my fifth year because I’m also minoring in Business Administration.

**LSL:** *“Your experience is quite rich.”*  
(All three professors tried to hide their smiles; I could tell they were genuinely happy to see me.)  
Throughout the interview, I reflected their own teachings back to them —  
the design puzzle, process thinking, and how design resolves problem structures.

# NYCU Interview Reflection

The Dean of the Interdisciplinary Arts program said only one sentence:  
> “You are suitable for design. Art is not for you.”

During the interview, I emphasized:  
> I want to create designs that help people —  
> something they can **feel**, something that **supports** them.
