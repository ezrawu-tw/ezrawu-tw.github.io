# Management

### What is a Business?
#### A Business is formed by an individual or a group of people

Main activities
* Acquiring and utilizing factors of production to conduct transactions and sales of products or services, satisfying consumer needs and generating profit in the process

#### Factors of Production

1. Natural resources
2. Human resources
    * Workers who contribute mental or physical labor
3. Capital equipment
    * Man-made {production tools}
4. Entrepreneurship
    * The spirit of starting and running a business

#### Products and Services That Satisfy Consumer Needs

1. Tangible products    
2. Intangible services -> Activities that benefit customers: the service industry

### Business Functions and Management Functions



| Business Functions | Management Functions | 
| -------- | -------- |
| Production     | Planning     | 
| Marketing     | Organizing     | 
| Human Resources     | Leading     | 
| Research & Development     | Controlling     | 
| Finance     |      | 
| Information     |      | 

Planning: Setting goals for future organizational performance

Organizing: Task assignment, departmentalization, and resource allocation
A process of detecting, comparing, evaluating, and correcting toward organizational goals


Leading: Using influence to motivate employees to achieve organizational goals

Controlling: Measuring the performance of employees or business units to ensure the realization of organizational plans or goals, and deciding whether to take corrective action as needed

### Managerial Roles

Interpersonal Roles
1. Figurehead
2. Leader
3. Liaison: Responding to emails
Informational Roles
1. Monitor
2. Disseminator
3. Spokesperson
Decisional Roles:
1. Entrepreneur
2. Disturbance Handler
3. Resource Allocator
4. Negotiator

### Effectiveness vs. Efficiency

Getting it right the first time with the same amount of effort
-> High efficiency and high effectiveness

Efficiency refers to the relationship between inputs and outputs

The two are not necessarily related to each other

### Skills

Conceptual skills: Managers must have the ability to solve problems and think critically in order to correctly analyze situations and make decisions
