#  After Experiencing the Silent Darkness of the Deep Sea, I Realized What the Meaning of Life Is!

Before the boat dive, I couldn't quite feel it —
Why am I here?
What am I supposed to be doing here?
Why am I confining myself to NYUST?

But after experiencing a world where everything around me was nothing but darkness, guided only by the faint beam of a flashlight through the deep sea,
and feeling nothing in that moment except the sound of my own breathing and my partner's —
everything that had felt meaningless
suddenly became meaningful.
Breathing, gestures, seawater, light, nature, fish — all of it became meaning.

Being alive is itself a meaning.

## Love Yourself. Live in the Present.

![](me.jpg)

Looking back, my dream has always been to enter the HCI field —
to work alongside a group of HCI researchers,
studying together how to use design
to uncover problems
rather than just finding straight-line answers.

And hey — I've already made that dream come true.
I'm now in the department I love.
For the first time, I'm face-to-face with the field I'm passionate about,
rather than having fate make the choice for me.

I love the air in Yunlin —
filled with the scent of soil and rice,
people riding bicycles,
and all the memories and stories that live there in my mind.

The clubs I once messed up.
The friends who went on adventures with me.

All of it is there.

Maybe the present moment is the meaning of life.


## The Story Goes Back To

![](friend.jpg)


On March 30, 2026, I skipped class to go learn how to dive.
At first, I just wanted to try it out — what is diving, really?
But diving is expensive.
So I decided to go ahead and get my PADI OW certification.
And just like that, I took action and headed to Xiao Liuqiu to get certified.

I worked through the online course materials
and passed the Open Water diver exam.

Later, I met a friend from Hong Kong named Franky.
I asked him if he wanted to do a night tour together.
He happily agreed.

On the way, we came across a turtle.
We helped save the turtle.

We had a meal together.
Simple. Peaceful. That too is the meaning of life.

## Advanced Open Water Certification

Franky mentioned that AOW allows you to see shipwrecks and do night dives.
That sparked my interest immediately.
So I signed up on the spot.
$1,000 USD — the best investment I've ever made.
Night diving. I finally experienced it.
Surrounded by still, cold seawater.
After turning off the flashlight,
everything faded into nothingness.
I moved my arms — stars flickered.
Turns out those were krill.
Turns out this is life.



## We Were Swept 2 Kilometers Out to Sea by the Current

On our first boat dive,
we were originally heading to see the Venice shipwreck.
But during our descent,
the current was too strong.
The instructor told us to let go of the rope
and let the current take us wherever it would.
So this is nature.
In awe, but not afraid — I think I've seen through what my life is.
When we surfaced and inflated our safety buoys,
we signaled the boat captain to come pick us up — only to realize that nature had carried us 2 km away into the open ocean.

## Falling in Love with Experience. Living in the Present.


![](coach.jpg)

I love this quote from The Secret Life of Walter Mitty:
'To see the world, things dangerous to come to, to see behind walls, draw closer, to find each other and to feel. That is the purpose of life.'
— *The Secret Life of Walter Mitty*

Starting now, I want to live in every single second.
No dwelling on the past.
No anxiety about the future.
Only paying for what I feel in the present.
