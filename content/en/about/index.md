Hi, I’m Yi-Sheng, but you can also call me Ezra.

Currently a graduate student in digital media and design, my academic journey bridges information management and creative media. I focus on Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), where I dedicate myself to developing wearable devices that monitor and visualize emotional states through physiological data. My long-term research goal is to publish my findings in top-tier international conferences like ACM CHI.

My passion for solving real-world problems through technology extends into social innovation and tech competitions. At 20, I collaborated with peers from Canada and India to co-found a social impact project and startup dedicated to blind children's education. Driven by hands-on tech implementation, I have also claimed top-three finishes in 10 different hackathons. To collaborate seamlessly with cross-cultural partners worldwide, I enjoy learning languages and can communicate across Mandarin, English, Spanish, German, and Thai.

Beyond coding and research, I thrive on pushing my boundaries. As an avid PADI Advanced Open Water (AOW) diver, I am fascinated by night diving and exploring the ocean depths in pitch darkness. For me, chasing faint glimmers of marine life underwater mirrors my work at the intersection of technology and design—constantly exploring and expanding the boundaries of human sensory experience.
