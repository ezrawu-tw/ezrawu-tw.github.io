After experiencing the silent, lightless deep sea, I finally understood the meaning of life!

Created2026-04-21|Updated2026-04-22|Good Start
|Word Count:471|Reading Time:2mins|Post Views:

After experiencing the silent, lightless deep sea, I finally understood the meaning of life!

Before the boat dive,
I couldn’t feel why I was here—
what I was meant to do,
why I had confined myself in YunTech.

But in the depths—
where darkness consumed everything,
and only a faint beam of light
guided the abyss—

All I could hear
was the rhythm of breath,
mine and my companions’,
echoing in the void.

And suddenly,
what once felt meaningless
became meaning itself.

Breath.
Gestures.
Seawater.
Light.
Nature.
Fish.

All of it—meaning.

To be alive,
is meaning enough.

Love yourself. Live in the present.

Looking back,
my dream was to enter the field of HCI—
to stand among researchers,
to explore through design,
not to chase linear answers,
but to uncover the questions themselves.

And then it struck me—
I’ve already made it.

I am here,
face to face with what I love,
not chosen by fate,
but chosen by myself.

I love the air of Yunlin—
the scent of soil and rice fields,
cyclists passing by,
and stories woven into memory.

The clubs I once failed,
the friends who once adventured with me—
they all remain there.

Perhaps
this very moment
is the meaning of life.

The story begins again

March 30, 2026—
I skipped class to learn diving.

At first,
just curiosity:
What is diving?

But diving is expensive.
So I thought—
why not go all in?

I went straight to Xiaoliuqiu
and took the PADI Open Water course.

I studied online,
passed the exam,
and then met Franky from Hong Kong.

I asked him,
“Want to go night diving?”

He smiled—
and said yes.

On the road,
we found a sea turtle,
saved it,
shared a meal.

Simple.
Peaceful.

And somehow,
that too—was meaning.

Advanced Open Water

Franky said:
“AOW lets you see shipwrecks… and night dives.”

That was enough.
I signed up instantly—
$1000.

The best investment I’ve ever made.

Night diving—
cold, silent water.
Lights off—

Everything returns
to nothingness.

I waved my arms—
stars shimmered.

Plankton.

So this—
this is life.

Carried away by the current

My first boat dive—
we were meant to see the Venetian shipwreck.

But halfway down,
the current grew too strong.

The instructor signaled:
“Let go.”

So we did.

And surrendered
to the ocean.

This is nature—
to be in awe,
but not in fear.

When we surfaced,
inflated our BCDs,
and waited for the boat—

we realized
the ocean had carried us
2 kilometers away.

Falling in love with experience, living in the present

I love this line:

“To see the world, things dangerous to come to,
to see behind walls, draw closer,
to find each other and to feel.
That is the purpose of life.”

To expand horizons,
to break through limits,
to see the world,
to come closer,
to feel—

That is life’s purpose.
