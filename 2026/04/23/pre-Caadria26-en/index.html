Discipline as a Lifestyle: Getting in Sync with the World for CAADRIA.

Translation is Not Enough

Bridging the Cognitive Gap
Many people say, “Just use body language,” or “Why not just use translation apps?” But as someone who has managed multinational teams, I know that is far from enough.

Language is more than words; it’s about pronunciation and regional nuances. Different countries have different vocabulary preferences, and even with the same language, misunderstandings happen. Moreover, one-to-one translation is a massive waste of time.

Take a joke from Rick and Morty, for example. If I have to ask someone to wait while I translate the punchline, it’s fundamentally a lack of respect for their culture. My deepest realization came from working with an Indian teammate; while every native speaker understood him perfectly, I was the only one struggling—despite being the leader he trusted most.

The Path to Change

Beyond intensive 1-on-1 tutoring, I’ve restructured my daily life:
Mornings: Starting the day with English listening practice.
Evenings: Chatting with random people on English Discord servers before bed.

Through this, I met KP and Nyear, two brilliant guys from top medical and polytechnic universities in Thailand. We’ve discussed everything from daily life and Thai politics to music and food. I’ve even learned how to argue and confess my feelings in English. It’s been an incredible journey.

My Filipino Tutor

My private tutor is from the Philippines. He specializes in debate and business English, which fits me perfectly. I’ve always loved how Rick in Rick and Morty roasts people with that lightning-fast delivery—it’s exhilarating to watch.

When I started, it had been a long time since I’d practiced (not since September 2025), but now, the words just flow. I’ve even started catching more pop culture references in American TV shows.

Some people mock me, saying I’m wasting time and money. But to me, nothing is ever truly a waste. Being alive means exploring everything you don’t yet know.

The Motivation

What started as a simple spark of motivation has turned into a two-month adventure:
all to prepare for my role as a conference assistant at CAADRIA in two months.
